Seattle King County Homelessness Coalition Site Redesign

To turn the passion to action for potential users on the non-profit site

Overview

The Seattle King County Coalition of Homelessness(SKCCH) is a local non-profit organization which advocates for the homelessness and educates public, provides trainings for advocates, and mobilizes the public.

Being one of the users, we tried to figure out how to get involved with organization. However, we had hard time understanding what services they provides and how we can commit. Thus, it was where we started our research and redesign journey in our User Research Method course.

Duration: 2.5 month
Roles: UX Designer
Tools: Illustrator / Sketch
Members: Jessica Pan, Kristina

1. Research

Competitive Analysis:
To analyze the approaches of the similar organizations that share the same group of audiences and goals, we chose 2 competitors- Facing Homelessness and Transition Projects. We uncovered the key point of views from 3 aspects: (1)Problem recognition, (2) Involvement, and (3) Results:


Contextual Interview:
After the competitive analysis, we conducted the contextual interviews to gather the general background from the users who are interested in social issues and uncovered their their pain points and expectations toward the experience, especially the homeless-related ones.

We have defined one high-level challenge the users are facing while they are getting involved with the non-profit organizations: it's easy to donate, hard to get further involvement.

According from the interviews, we were taking those insights to our following design:

  • Clearly demonstrate the programs: Since users pointed out they are barely taking any action if they do not know about the program, it is the top task for us to make all programs displayed clearer and more accessible.
  • Highlight the issue on the home page: Users are normally hooked by the first impression of the site and they care both the emotional look and feel and the statistics.
  • Indicate the program progress: A lots of visitors are result-oriented and will commit their time or donate money only when they see the past positive results.

Baseline Testing:
In order to measure the benchmark for the current site experience and allow to set the priorities, we created 6 tasks for the testing, measured chosen KPIs, and accumulated results to compare them with future changes.

(1) Most users couldn’t find an educational project because didn’t understand the label “Project Cool”

(2) A few users succeeded the task because they found another link “Back to school”

(3) Most users couldn’t find “Project Cool Report” because it looks like an add, instead of a report section

(4) Nobody navigated the site through the top navbar

"The CTA positioned on the top with the blue font color is really unclear” -tester

Ideation

Card-sorting:
At the first stage of the ideation process, we are to observe how users organize the topics into categories that make sense to them. By saying this, the site structure is in advanced reorganized based on the previous research result and divided into the cards. Then, we have 12 users to sort out the cards via OptimalSort or in person. Below are the takeaways we are going to apply to our final design:

  • Simplify the redundant content in “What We Do” and “Get Involved” page
  • Move “Become a Coalition Employee” to “Get Involved” page
  • Provide a brief introduction to all projects of the organization in a clearer way

Paper Prototype and Usability Test:
After having a solid understanding of the users, we were heading to the phase of visualizing. The paper prototyping is to help us learn deeper about how users interact with the interface and if the redesign prototypes provide more user-friendly experience.

Reflection

  • To make the user think out loud completely, the moderator should ASK them what they are actually thinking when there is no action taken by them, instead of giving hints
  • To make the site even more user-friendly for the targeted users, it will be more accurate to test the prototype with the SKCCH users
