MENU

Seattle King County Homelessness Coalition Site Redesign

To turn the passion to action on the non-profit website

Overview

The Seattle King County Coalition of Homelessness(SKCCH) is a local non-profit organization which advocates for the homeless and educates public, provides trainings for advocates and service providers, and mobilizes members and supporters. Being one of the users, we tried to figure out how to get involved with organization. However, we had hard time understanding what services they provides and how we can commit. Thus, it was where I started our research and redesign journey in a User Research Design Course.

2017 - Present
Roles: Researcher / UX Designer
Tools: Illustrator / Sketch

Research

Competitive Analysis:
To analyze the approaches of the organizations that share the same audience or goals, we chose 2 competitors for analysis-Facing Homelessness and Transition Projects. We uncovered the key point of views from 3 aspects: (1)Problem recognition, (2) Involvement, and (3) Results:

Contextual Interview:
After the competitive analysis, we conducted the contextual interview to gather the general background from the users who are interested in social issue and uncover their their pain points and expectations toward the non-profit organizations, especially the homeless-related ones.

We have defined one key challenge the users are facing while they are getting involved with the non-profit organization: it's easy to donate, hard to get further involvement. According from what we learned from the users, we are taking those insights to our following design:

  • Clearly demonstrate the programs: Making all programs more clear and accessible from the main page and highlighting their call to actions
  • Highlight the issue on the home page: Sharing both the homeless stories and photos for emotional attachment and the results of the programs for data-driven people
  • Indicate the program progress: Listing the progressive or successful statistic or stories of the programs

Baseline Testing:
In order to measures the benchmark for the current site experience and allows to set the priorities, we created 6 tasks for the testing, measured chosen KPIs, and accumulated results to compare them with future changes.

(1) Most users couldn’t find an educational project because didn’t understand the label “Project Cool”

Ideation

Card-sorting:
At the first stage of the ideation process, we are to observes users organizing topics into categories that make sense to them. By saying this, the site structure is reorganized based on the previous research result and divided into the cards. Then, we have 12 users to sort out the cards via OptimalSort or in person. Below are the takeaway we are going to apply to our design:

  • Simplify the redundant content in “What We Do” and “Get Involved” page
  • Add “Become a Coalition Employee” to “Get Involved” page
  • Provide a brief introduction to all projects of the organization

Paper Prototype and Usability Test:
After having a solid understanding of the users, we are heading to the phase of visualizing and test ideas and concepts. Further, the paper prototyping is helping us to learn deeper about how users interact with the interface and if the redesign prototypes provide more user-friendly experience.

Reflection

  • To optimize the testing experience, we will set up a camera tripod for the video recording, instead of manual recording. By doing this, the team members can fully act as a better “browser”
  • To make the user think out loud completely, the moderator should ask them what they are actually thinking when there is no action taken by them, instead of giving hints
  • To make the site even more user-friendly for the targeted users, it will be more accurate to test the prototype with the existing users
  • To create a more user-focus product, we will need to come up with the user persona after the research so that it is easier to follow persona’s need.
